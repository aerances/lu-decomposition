LU Decomposition (Doolittle Method)

1. Mathematical Discussion

LU Decomposition factorizes a given square coefficient matrix \(A\) into a product of two companion triangular matrices: a Lower Triangular Matrix (\(L\)) and an Upper Triangular Matrix (\(U\)), such that:

$$A = LU$$

In the Doolittle algorithm variant, the diagonal elements of matrix \(L\) are equal to 1:

$$l_{ii} = 1 \quad \text{for all } i$$

Doolittle's method provides an alternative way to factorize \(A\) into an LU decomposition directly without going through the process of Gaussian Elimination. The parameters are systematically solved from the linear system mappings column-by-column and row-by-row using the following linear algebraic formulations:

  • Upper Matrix Elements (\(U\)): For all \(j\), if \(i = 0\) $$u_{ij} = a_{ij}$$ For \(i > 0\) $$u_{ij} = a_{ij} - \sum_{k=0}^{i-1} l_{ik}u_{kj}$$
  • Lower Matrix Elements (\(L\)): For all \(i\), if \(j = 0\) $$l_{ij} = \frac{a_{ij}}{u_{jj}}$$ For \(j > 0\) $$l_{ij} = \frac{ a_{ij} - \sum_{k=0}^{j-1} l_{ik}u_{kj} }{ u_{jj} }$$

Applications of the Doolittle Algorithm

  1. Solving Linear Equations: LU decomposition simplifies solving \(AX=B\) by splitting it into two simpler systems: solving \(LY=B\) using forward substitution, then solving \(UX=Y\) using backward substitution.
  2. Matrix Inversion: LU decomposition can be used to compute the inverse of a matrix efficiently.
  3. Determinant Calculation: The determinant of matrix \(A\) can be obtained from the product of the diagonal elements of matrix \(U\).

2. Step-by-Step Worked Examples

Worked Example 1

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 & 1 \\ 4 & 7 & 4 \\ 6 & 11 & 10 \end{pmatrix}$$
\(u_{11}=2,\quad u_{12}=3,\quad u_{13}=1\)
\(l_{21}=\frac{4}{2}=2\)
\(l_{31}=\frac{6}{2}=3\)
\(u_{22}=7-(2\times3)=1\)
\(u_{23}=4-(2\times1)=2\)
\(l_{32}=\frac{11-(3\times3)}{1}=2\)
\(u_{33}=10-(3\times1+2\times2)=3\)

Final Matrices:

$$L = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$ $$U = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$$

Worked Example 2

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 4 \\ 3 & 8 & 14 \\ 2 & 6 & 13 \end{pmatrix}$$
\(u_{11}=1,\quad u_{12}=2,\quad u_{13}=4\)
\(l_{21}=\frac{3}{1}=3\)
\(l_{31}=\frac{2}{1}=2\)
\(u_{22}=8-(3\times2)=2\)
\(u_{23}=14-(3\times4)=2\)
\(l_{32}=\frac{6-(2\times2)}{2}=1\)
\(u_{33}=13-(2\times4+1\times2)=3\)

Final Matrices:

$$L = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$ $$U = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$$
